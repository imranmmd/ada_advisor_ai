4211. Merit-Based Scholarships for Incoming Undergraduate Students 
1.1. These scholarships are awarded to incoming undergraduate students for their 
outstanding academic performance prior to being enrolled at ADA University. 
1.2. Scholarships awarded to incoming undergraduate students who take an academic leave 
right after their admission to ADA University will remain in students’ accounts for the 
period of academic leave for maximum 2 years. The scholarship amount will be 
applied to the first term of study when the student returns to register for the classes. 
1.3. Undergraduate students who are awarded with scholarships for their outstanding 
academic performance prior to being enrolled at ADA University are eligible to 
maintain their scholarships for the semester following their exchange program abroad 
upon successfully completing their exchange study. 
1.4. Information about scholarships available for incoming undergraduate students is 
presented in Annex 1. 
4212. Merit-Based Scholarships for Returning Undergraduate Students 
2.1. Finance Office announces a list of students who receives honors and distinctions at the 
end of each semester. Top 20 percent of each undergraduate class gets included on 
these lists and receives scholarships. 
2.2. Students must maintain a full-load coursework by earned hours and must have 
minimum term GPA 3.0 to be eligible for Merit Based Scholarships. 
2.3. Students found in violation of the ADA University Honor Code once will not be 
eligible for scholarship for the remainder of their tenure at the University. 
2.4. If a student has one failing grade (F, NP) in the given semester, he/she is not eligible 
for merit-based scholarship. 
2.5. Cumulative GPA may be referred to when two or more students have the same GPA. 
2.6. Scholarship is awarded by GPA in effect as of the day of final grades due date. 
2.7. Grades of undergraduate students studying in an exchange program at another 
institution are not calculated in GPA. Therefore, they are not considered for a merit-
based scholarship. 
2.8. Students who are awarded with scholarships are provided by the Certificate of 
Achievement from their School side.

2.9. Students are eligible to earn only one scholarship and/or exemption per semester. 
The scholarship and/or exemption which provides the highest tuition waiver for a 
student is awarded each semester. 
2.10. Scholarships awarded to returning undergraduate students who take an academic 
leave in the subsequent semester of the scholarship earned will remain in students’ 
accounts for the period of academic leave for maximum 2 years. The scholarship 
amount will be applied to the first term of study when the student returns to register 
for the classes. Merit-based scholarships are not transferable across semesters in all 
other circumstances. 
2.11. Information about scholarships available for returning undergraduate students is 
presented in Annex 2.
