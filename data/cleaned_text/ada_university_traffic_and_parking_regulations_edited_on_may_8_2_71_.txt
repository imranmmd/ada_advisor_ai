ADA University 
Traffic and Parking Regulations 
 
1. Purpose 
The Traffic and Parking Regulations of ADA University aim to ensure the safety and well-being of 
students, employees, and visitors; to safeguard and properly utilize University property; and to 
facilitate the efficient operation of the University. 
2. Application 
These regulations apply to all students and employees of the University and are integral to the terms 
and conditions of student admission and enrollment, as well as full-time and part-time employment. 
Additionally, these regulations apply to all service contractor and visitors and form part of the terms 
and conditions for granting permission to enter ADA University grounds. The operation of a motor 
vehicle on the ADA University campus by any individual signifies their agreement to comply with 
and be bound by these regulations. 
3. Limitation of Liability 
ADA University is not responsible for any loss or damage to vehicles and their belongings from any 
cause while they are on ADA University grounds. 
 
4. Traffic Regulations 
General Traffic Regulations 
• 
The maximum speed limit on campus is 10 km/h. All drivers must adhere to this limit to 
ensure the safety of pedestrians and other vehicles. 
• 
Vehicle license plates must always be kept clean and clearly visible. 
• 
All drivers on campus must obey posted traffic signs, signals, and road markings to ensure 
smooth traffic flow and maintain safety. One-way routes, speed limits, stop signs, and 
designated parking zones must be strictly followed. 
• 
Pedestrians must always use designated crosswalks and pedestrian pathways. Crossing 
outside of marked areas may not be safe and can increase the risk of accidents. Drivers must 
slow down and yield to pedestrians crossing the road. 
 
5. Parking Regulations 
Parking Rules 
• 
Vehicles must be parked within marked spaces and should not obstruct traffic flow, pedestrian 
pathways, or emergency access routes. 
• 
Parking on campus is permitted only in designated parking areas:

o 
Building A underground parking: Only for full-time staff, faculty, and special 
guests. 
o 
Building B underground parking: Only for guests and part-time/adjunct faculty. 
o 
Building E underground parking: Only for full-time staff and faculty.

Building E open-air parking: For students, staff, faculty, and guests. 
• 
Parking access hours are from 07:00 to 23:00. All vehicles must vacate the parking area by 
23:00. 
• 
If a vehicle must be parked overnight, prior notice must be given, and the car keys must be 
handed over to the Parking Office. 
• 
If someone parks in a designated space without authorization, it must be reported immediately 
to the Parking Office. 
• 
Unauthorized parking in restricted zones, including fire lanes, loading zones, and reserved 
spaces, is strictly prohibited. 
• 
Bicycle parking slots are available in Building E open-air parking (for students) and 
Building B underground parking (for staff) 
Parking Permits 
• 
Staff and faculty permits are processed through the Office of Organisation and Personnel 
upon receiving a full-time contract. 
• 
Student permits (limited availability): Students can only use open-air parking spaces upon 
registration. To register, students must contact the Office of Student Services. 
• 
Adjunct faculty permits (limited availability): Adjunct faculty should contact the Office of 
Organisation and Personnel to register their vehicle. 
• 
Visitor permits (short-term): The list of potential visitors, event attendees, and their vehicle 
license plates should be submitted to the Welcome Center in advance. 
• 
Special permits (accessible parking, service vehicles, contractors): A list should be 
submitted to the Welcome Center prior to arrival. 
 
6. Drop-off and Pick-up Regulations 
ADA University Drop-off and Pick-up Areas 
• 
Main Entrance: The designated drop-off and pick-up area is in front of the Main Welcome 
Center. 
• 
Vehicles entering this area must follow traffic signs, move to the first available free spot, and 
stop only for drop-off or pick-up. 
• 
Passengers must exit the vehicle from the safe side facing the sidewalk.

• 
No waiting is allowed in this area. Vehicles must leave immediately after drop-off/pick-up. 
Waiting for more than 1 minute will result in penalties. 
• 
If the area is full, vehicles must not enter and should proceed further and return for another 
attempt. 
ADA High School Drop-off and Pick-up Area 
• 
Building E open-air parking lot is the designated area for ADA High School. 
 
Students, employees, and visitors may also use other safe parking zones near the campus for drop-off 
and pick-up. 
 
7. Enforcement and Penalties 
Compliance and Enforcement 
ADA University reserves the right to enforce parking regulations to ensure safety, accessibility, and 
efficient use of parking facilities. Parking in restricted zones or without a valid permit is strictly 
prohibited. Violators may be subject to fines, towing at the owner’s expense, or revocation of parking 
privileges. Repeated violations may result in further disciplinary action, including suspension of 
campus parking access. 
Penalties 
The premises of ADA University fall under the jurisdiction of the Main State Traffic Police 
Department of the Republic of Azerbaijan and the internal regulations of ADA University. 
State Traffic Police Oversight: 
• 
The Main State Traffic Police Department provides road signs and monitoring cameras to 
enforce national traffic laws. 
• 
Violations of national traffic laws will result in fines or, if necessary, vehicle evacuation. 
Parking Drop-off/Pick-up Violations: 
Students, employees, and service contractors violating regulations in designated parking, drop-off, 
and pick-up areas will face the following consequences: 
• 
First Violation: An oral warning will be issued. 
• 
Second Violation: A written warning will be given along with a fine of 50 AZN for each 
occurrence. 
• 
Subsequent Violations: Additional fines of 50 AZN per occurrence will be applied, and 
disciplinary measures will be escalated depending on the severity and frequency of the 
infractions.

• 
Drivers, including taxis and personal drivers transporting students and employees, must 
strictly comply with campus drop-off and pick-up regulations. Students and employees are 
accountable for ensuring that their drivers follow these rules. Repeated violations may result 
in disciplinary action against the passenger and potential restriction of campus access for the 
associated driver. 
 
8. Public Awareness and Education 
Traffic Education Programs 
ADA University is committed to raising awareness of traffic safety through education programs and 
materials provided to students, employees, and visitors. These initiatives include: 
• 
Information on the importance of following the university’s traffic regulations. 
• 
Promoting safe driving practices and responsible parking behavior. 
• 
Distribution of flyers, posters, and digital content on traffic safety measures, including the 
dangers of speeding, reckless driving, and distracted driving. 
9. Collaboration with State Traffic Police 
ADA University will work closely with the Main State Traffic Police Department to ensure the 
effective implementation of traffic enforcement systems, including the placement of road signs and 
surveillance cameras.
